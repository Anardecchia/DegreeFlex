Common Questions

What You Need to Know About the AMT Program

What does an Aviation Maintenance Technician do?

AMTs inspect, repair, and maintain aircraft to ensure they meet safety and performance standards. They work on airframes, powerplants, electrical systems, and hydraulics ‚Äî troubleshooting issues, replacing components, and performing routine maintenance to keep aircraft flying safely.

How long does the program take?

The Aviation Maintenance Technician program at AIM is 21 months for full-time students, with 78 credit hours total. The program runs continuously so you can get into your career faster.

Do I need a license to work as an aircraft maintenance technician?

Most employers prefer ‚Äî or require ‚Äî FAA certification in Airframe and Powerplant (A&P). AIM's program is specifically designed to prepare you for those exams, including written, oral, and practical components.

What's the salary potential for aviation maintenance technicians?

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median salary for A&P technicians is approximately $75,000 per year, with higher earnings available based on experience, certifications, and specialization.

What kinds of aircraft will I work on after graduating?

Aviation maintenance technicians can work on commercial airplanes, helicopters, cargo aircraft, and private planes depending on their specialization and certifications. The broader your certifications, the more opportunities you'll have.
