About the Program

Where Rigor Meets Real-World Application

In the Department of Computer and Data Sciences, you'll be surrounded by top faculty dedicated to deepening your knowledge of computer science and preparing you for a career that matters. Through this 30-credit-hour program, you gain experiential and applied learning through co-ops and research tailored to your interests.

You can choose to pursue either a course-focused or research-focused master's degree — building the exact foundation your career requires, whether that's industry or academia.

"I valued working with professors on interesting projects for my final project requirement. Being given the opportunity to build them from scratch and have them serve useful purposes made the work fulfilling while also emulating real-world software development."
Mohammad Doleh (CSE '17) · Software Developer Engineer, Expedia Group

Admissions

How to Apply

Case Western Reserve's graduate admissions process is straightforward. Here's what you'll need to submit to be considered for the M.S. in Computer Science:

  • Completed online application
  • Background knowledge in data structures, algorithms, and operating systems
  • Official transcripts from all prior institutions
  • Three letters of recommendation
  • GRE test scores
  • TOEFL or IELTS scores (international students)
