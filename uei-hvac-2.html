Program Overview

What You'll Learn at UEI

UEI's HVAC Technician Training Program provides the knowledge, skills, and hands-on training needed for an entry-level HVAC career. You'll work with real systems in the lab — not just textbooks — so you're ready for the field from day one.

The program prepares you for two important industry certifications: the EPA Section 608 Certification (required for graduation) and the Low GWP Refrigerant Safety Certification. HVAC systems have become highly complex, and employers increasingly prefer candidates with post-secondary training who can handle modern residential and commercial equipment.

Installation & Maintenance

Install, maintain, troubleshoot, and repair residential and commercial HVAC systems

Electricity & Controls

Electrical components, household wiring, series/parallel circuits, HVAC control devices

Refrigeration Systems

Basic refrigeration principles, refrigerant handling, system evacuation and charging

Heating Systems

Gas, electric, and oil heating in residential applications; combustion principles

Duct Design & Blueprint Reading

Air distribution, ductwork sizing, load calculations, blueprint interpretation

Commercial Refrigeration

Commercial equipment components, installation, defrost cycles, safety protocols

Curriculum

9 Course Modules

Each module builds progressively toward a complete foundation in HVAC systems — from basic electrical theory to commercial equipment and EPA certification prep.

  1. Basic Electricity, Motors, and HVAC Controls
  2. Basic Refrigeration Theory and Application
  3. Air Conditioning, Troubleshooting and Heat Pump Systems
  4. Heating Systems
  5. Duct Design, Blueprint Reading, and Load Calculation
  6. Commercial Refrigeration Systems
  7. Specialized Commercial Equipment and Green Awareness
  8. Regulation and Building Automation
  9. Electrical and Mechanical Troubleshooting
