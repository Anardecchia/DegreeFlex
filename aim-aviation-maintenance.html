What You'll Train For

Aviation Maintenance Programs

AIM's programs prepare you for FAA certification as an Aviation Maintenance Technician — one of the most in-demand credentials in the aviation industry. Train on real aircraft with instructors who've worked in the field.

✈️
Airframe & Powerplant (A&P)

The gold standard in aviation maintenance. Earn both FAA certifications and qualify to maintain virtually any commercial or private aircraft. This comprehensive program covers airframe structures, aircraft systems, and powerplant overhaul.

FAA Certification Day & Evening Classes Financial Aid Available
🔧
Airframe Technician

Focus on aircraft structures, hydraulic systems, flight controls, and landing gear. Graduate ready to specialize in airframe inspection and repair for airlines, MRO facilities, and private operators.

FAA Part 147 Hands-On Labs
⚙️
Powerplant Technician

Specialize in aircraft engines — from reciprocating engines to turbines. Learn overhaul, inspection, and troubleshooting procedures used by airline maintenance departments and engine shops.

Turbine Training Real Engine Labs
📡
Avionics Technology

The fastest-growing area of aviation maintenance. Work with aircraft electronics, navigation systems, communications equipment, and autopilot systems as aircraft become increasingly technology-driven.

Where You'll Go

Career Outcomes After AIM

Aviation Maintenance Technicians are in critical demand. The FAA projects a shortage of over 40,000 technicians in the next decade — AIM graduates are positioned to fill those roles.

🛫
Commercial Airlines

Maintain commercial jets for major carriers including American, Delta, United, and Southwest. Line, base, and heavy maintenance roles available.

🏭
MRO Facilities

Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul shops handle aircraft from carriers worldwide. High volume, specialized work, and strong pay.

🪖
Military / DoD

Many AMTs transition into Department of Defense contractor roles maintaining military aircraft — often with premium pay packages.

🏢
Corporate Aviation

Business jet operators, fractional ownership fleets, and charter operators need skilled AMTs to keep their aircraft airworthy.

🔬
Aircraft Manufacturing

Boeing, Airbus, and their suppliers hire AMT-certified technicians for production, quality assurance, and final assembly roles.

📋
FAA Inspection

With experience, A&P holders can pursue Inspection Authorization (IA) certification — the highest individual credential in aircraft maintenance.
